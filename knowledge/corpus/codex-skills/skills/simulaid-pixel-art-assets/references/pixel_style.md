# Simulaid Pixel Style

## Pixel Grid

- Item, seed, card, enemy, resource, and loot icons: final `256x256` RGBA PNG with transparent background. Use high-quality source art and roughly `96px` to `128px` logical pixel detail scaled with nearest-neighbor when the asset is important or large in UI. Use `64px` to `96px` only for simpler small resources.
- Crop growth sprites: final `256x256` RGBA PNG with transparent background. Each stage should eventually have dry/wet variants named `crop_{cropId}_{stage}_dry` and `crop_{cropId}_{stage}_wet`. All stages for one crop must share the same bottom baseline inside the transparent canvas so seeds, sprouts, and mature plants grow upward without visually jumping.
- Farm plot sprites: final `512x512` RGBA PNG with transparent background. Use a strict 1:1 top-down square tile and roughly `128px` logical detail scaled with nearest-neighbor.
- Main-menu/background scenes can be opaque larger pixel-informed images, sized for the Unity UI target rather than icon format.
- Preserve hard pixel edges and readable clusters. Avoid soft-focus blur, painterly haze, vector-flat placeholders, photorealistic product renders, cheap MMORPG glow, or detail that vanishes on a phone.
- Companion/enemy/character animation frames should use the same refined standard as approved item icons and zombie sprites. They must not look like simple doodles, flat code art, or disconnected per-frame redraws.

## Quality Bar

- "Pixel style" means high-definition pixel-informed art: strong silhouette, deliberate square-pixel clusters, crisp hard edges, and controlled palette. It must not look low-resolution, smeared, rough, or autogenerated from simple geometric shapes.
- Do not make assets "more pixel" by blurring them. Pixelization should be nearest-neighbor/blocky, not camera blur, fog, watercolor softness, or mushy compression.
- For user-visible card and item icons, generate/approve one asset at a time when the style is not already approved. Do not batch-generate a whole set from an unapproved prompt.
- Final Unity sprites should be clean cutouts with generous padding, no baked UI frame, no text, no watermark, and no chroma-key fringe.
- Procedural fallback art from scripts is not acceptable as completed player-facing art. If image/animation generation is failed, busy, or unavailable, a rough session placeholder may be used to keep code/layout work moving, but it must be clearly temporary and outside final delivery.

## Alignment And Frame QA

- For every transparent sprite, compute the alpha bounding box and inspect visual center, padding, and bottom baseline before import.
- Related assets must be compared as a set. Crops should grow upward from the same baseline; enemies and companions should not shift when variants or animation frames change.
- For sprite sheets, split frames by the exact grid first, then validate each frame:
  - no empty frame,
  - no clipped subject,
  - consistent padding,
  - stable bottom contact point,
  - stable center/centroid unless the whole action intentionally moves.
- Idle companion loops should feel alive through small internal motion only: breathing/sway, wagging tail, blinking, or tongue movement. The body mass should not translate across the cell.
- Use contact-sheet or bounding-box overlays to inspect first/middle/last frames before Unity wiring. If a sheet looks like jitter, twitching, or misaligned crops, regenerate or re-register the frames.

## Palette

Use a restrained palette compatible with the Simulaid UI:

- outline: `#0a1220`
- navy: `#0e1d34`
- panel blue: `#1c5382`
- cyan highlight: `#61b5db`
- blue-white text/light: `#def1ff`
- gold loot: `#e2b03e`
- orange warning: `#be5c2d`
- red danger: `#a4303e`
- crop green: `#4a9a54`
- dry farm soil: dominant `#724523`, with highlights near `#805020` / `#906030` and dark pixels near `#503010`
- wet farm soil: dominant `#3d2210`, with clusters near `#402010` / `#503010` and sparse cool wet highlights
- uncultivated soil: darker than dry usable soil; use packed earth, stones, twigs, and sparse weeds
- purple rare: `#6753a8`
- steel: `#718497`

## Weapon And Relic Direction

- Survival equipment such as `消防斧子`, `锤子`, `开山刀`, and `手枪` can be worn and practical, but still stylized as premium inventory art rather than realistic catalog photography.
- Sacred legendary/mythic weapons such as `倚天剑` and `人皇剑` must be clean, graceful, and holy. Use restrained glow, pale jade, moonlit silver, warm white, subtle cyan, and tiny muted-gold accents.
- Avoid noisy web-game cues for sacred weapons: oversized spikes, overfilled ornaments, neon/rainbow beams, heavy gold plating, huge particle explosions, and aggressive "loot drop" shine.
- `倚天剑`: cool white/silver blade, faint cyan edge, pale jade or brushed-silver hilt, light cloud-pattern guard, slender ancient straight-sword silhouette.
- `人皇剑`: more ceremonial and sovereign than flashy; warm white, jade-gold, engraved but uncluttered hilt, calm divine aura, dignified straight-sword silhouette.

## Conversion Guidance

For AI or large-source images generated through `imagegen`:

1. Remove/cut out the subject first if needed.
2. Crop square with generous padding.
3. Pixelize to the correct logical grid for the asset family, then scale to the final PNG size with nearest-neighbor.
4. Manually or script-clean chroma-key fringe and silhouette artifacts.
5. For farm plots and crop overlays, keep canvas size, visual center, bottom baseline, and soil palette consistent between dry/wet/locked states.
6. For animation sheets, run frame-registration QA before importing: split frames, measure alpha boxes/centroids/baselines, correct drift with transparent padding, and preview the contact sheet.
7. Compare the result against the approved direction before importing: no photorealism, no blur, no cheap web-game effects, no rough placeholder look, no jittery frame crops.
8. Import/use through Unity Resources and verify in the Unity UI.

Temporary placeholders created because generation is failed/busy/unavailable may skip the artistic quality bar, but not the naming/reporting rule. They should still preserve target dimensions and importability so they do not create false code bugs.

Do not use paid background-removal or upscaling services unless the user explicitly approves.
